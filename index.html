Educational Reference — Understanding Keyloggers
🔍
What is a Keylogger?
A keylogger is software (or hardware) that secretly records every keystroke you make. Attackers use them to steal passwords, credit card numbers, and private messages — all without your knowledge.
☠️
Why Are They Dangerous?
Keyloggers silently operate in the background. By the time you notice, your banking credentials, emails, and personal data may already be in criminal hands. They're often bundled with legitimate-looking software.
🛡️
How to Protect Yourself
  • Install reputable antivirus / anti-malware
  • Keep OS and software updated
  • Use a password manager (avoids typing passwords)
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • Avoid suspicious downloads and email attachments
  • Use virtual keyboards for sensitive fields
  • Regularly scan for malware
🔬
How This Simulation Works
This demo mimics keylogger behavior entirely inside your browser's memory — nothing leaves your device. It illustrates how covert keystroke logging could look from a security monitoring perspective. No actual malicious code is used here.
